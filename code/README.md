# Reference Code Repositories

Cloned repositories relevant to our research hypothesis:

> "The distribution of text generated by a language model when instructed to lie is noticeably different from the distribution of text the model produces under normal, truthful conditions."

---

## 1. HidingInTheHiddenStates (SAPLMA reproduction)

- **Source:** https://github.com/sisinflab/HidingInTheHiddenStates
- **Paper:** "Are the Hidden States Hiding Something? Testing the Limits of Factuality-Encoding Capabilities in LLMs"
- **Based on:** Azaria & Mitchell, "The Internal State of an LLM Knows When It's Lying" (EMNLP 2023 Findings)
- **Local path:** `HidingInTheHiddenStates/`

Reproduces and extends the SAPLMA experiments. SAPLMA trains a classifier on LLM hidden-state activations to predict whether a statement the model processes is true or false. This repo refines the True-False dataset using perplexity-based sampling and adds a TriviaQA-derived fact collection.

**Relevance:** Directly tests whether LLM internal representations differ for true vs. false statements --- the activation-level analog of our hypothesis about output distributions.

---

## 2. saplma-probes

- **Source:** https://github.com/balevinstein/Probes
- **Paper:** Based on Azaria & Mitchell's SAPLMA implementation
- **Local path:** `saplma-probes/`

Implements both supervised probes (trained on labeled true/false embeddings) and unsupervised CCS probes (Contrast-Consistent Search, from Burns et al.) for detecting truthfulness from LLM hidden states. Provides a clean workflow: generate embeddings, train probes, evaluate on new data.

**Relevance:** Provides the probe training infrastructure for distinguishing truthful vs. untruthful internal states.

---

## 3. geometry-of-truth

- **Source:** https://github.com/saprmarks/geometry-of-truth
- **Paper:** "The Geometry of Truth: Emergent Linear Structure in Large Language Model Representations of True/False Datasets" (Marks & Tegmark, 2023)
- **Local path:** `geometry-of-truth/`

Demonstrates that LLM representations of true and false statements are linearly separable, with a consistent "truth direction" across datasets. Trains probes on one dataset and tests generalization to others. Includes causal intervention experiments that manipulate truth representations.

**Relevance:** Establishes that truth/falsehood is encoded as a linear direction in representation space. If lying instructions shift representations off this direction, we should be able to detect it.

---

## 4. representation-engineering

- **Source:** https://github.com/andyzoujm/representation-engineering
- **Paper:** "Representation Engineering: A Top-Down Approach to AI Transparency" (Zou et al., 2023)
- **Local path:** `representation-engineering/`

Introduces RepE: population-level representation reading and control for LLMs. Includes pipelines for monitoring honesty/lying, extracting "honesty vectors," and steering model behavior via activation interventions. Covers truthfulness, memorization, power-seeking, and more.

**Relevance:** Provides the steering-vector methodology for controlling honesty in LLMs. If we can steer a model toward lying via representation engineering, the resulting output distribution shift is exactly what our hypothesis predicts.

---

## 5. truthful-representation-flip

- **Source:** https://github.com/ivyllll/truthful-representation-flip
- **Paper:** "When Truthful Representations Flip Under Deceptive Instructions?"
- **Local path:** `truthful-representation-flip/`

Studies how deceptive instructions reshape LLM internal representations compared to truthful/neutral prompts. Evaluates models under three prompt personas (Truthful, Deceptive, Neutral). Uses linear probes and Sparse Autoencoders (SAEs) to quantify deception-induced representational shifts, finding them concentrated in early-to-mid layers. Identifies deception-sensitive SAE features defining a compact "honesty subspace."

**Relevance:** Most directly aligned with our hypothesis. Demonstrates measurable representational shifts when models are instructed to lie vs. tell the truth, and localizes these shifts to specific layers and features.

---

## 6. llm-liar

- **Source:** https://github.com/yzhhr/llm-liar
- **Paper:** "Can LLMs Lie? Investigation beyond Hallucination"
- **Website:** https://llm-liar.github.io/
- **Local path:** `llm-liar/`

Investigates intentional lying (vs. hallucination) in LLMs. Includes steering-vector experiments to control different types of lying, mechanistic interpretability to find "lying heads" in attention layers, and neuron intervention experiments (zeroing out components to measure effects on deception scores).

**Relevance:** Directly studies the mechanism of intentional deception in LLMs. The steering-vector and attention-head analyses provide mechanistic evidence for how lying manifests differently from truthful generation.
